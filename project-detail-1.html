to Design
This dating app is designed to encourage meaningful interactions between users. It puts less emphasis on profile pictures, and more on conversations. The concept is that images remain hidden until users have exchanged a few messages. The goal is to motivate people to discover shared interests and build connections, rather than basing their
attraction on appearances alone. The idea is inspired by a concept I came across in psychology, which, in simple terms, suggests that we’re often attracted to those we perceive as similar to ourselves. But how can we ever know who is similar to us if we don't get to know each other?
One major issue with many existing dating apps is that they’re intentionally built to keep users engaged for as long as possible, as this maximizes their revenue. This became especially clear after I listened to an episode of P3 Dystopia on online dating. The episode discussed how these platforms are designed to manipulate user behavior
in an unethical manner. They’re not necessarily focused on helping people find real connections, but rather on keeping them swiping, scrolling, and constantly coming back for more. This became the starting point for me when I began thinking about solutions.
